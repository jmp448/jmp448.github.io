Biography

I am a PhD student in Alexis Battle’s lab at Johns Hopkins University, in the Department of Biomedical Engineering. I completed my undergraduate training at Cornell University in the Department of Biological Engineering. While there, I completed an honors thesis under Dan Luo and Shogo Hamada, working to combine the genetic and material properties of DNA to develop a highly sensitive and specific diagnostic device for point-of-care settings. I also worked in Ed Buckler’s plant computational genomics lab, striving to improve high-throughput phenotyping methods to facilitate the study of genotype-phenotype interactions.

Here at Johns Hopkins, my research focus is on applying machine learning methods to look at the dynamic landscape of genetic regulation of gene expression. If you’re interested in hearing more, with or without the scientific jargon, check out the research tab!
